## 2Ô∏è‚É£ QA vs QC

| Aspect         | QA (Quality Assurance)         | QC (Quality Control)                   |
| -------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Definition     | Process to **prevent defects** | Process to **identify defects**        |
| Focus          | Process improvement            | Product quality                        |
| Type           | Preventive                     | Detective                              |
| Activities     | Audits, process check, reviews | Testing, defect detection              |
| Responsibility | QA team                        | QC team / Testers                      |
| Timing         | Throughout SDLC                | After product is developed             |
| Goal           | Ensure quality in process      | Ensure quality in product              |
| Example        | ISO process checks, standards  | Functional testing, regression testing |
- ‚úÖ Expert Tip: QA is process-focused, QC is product-focused.

# 10 Key Differences ‚Äì QA & QC, Verification & Validation

1. Verification is static; Validation is dynamic.
2. Verification ensures process correctness; Validation ensures product correctness.
3. QA prevents defects; QC detects defects.
4. QA is process-focused; QC is product-focused.
5. Verification done during development; Validation done after development.
6. QA involves process audits; QC involves testing activities.
7. QA responsibility: QA team; QC responsibility: Testers.
8. QA improves process; QC ensures quality in software.
9. Verification asks "Did we do it right?"; Validation asks "Did we build the right thing?"
10. QA is preventive; QC is detective.
